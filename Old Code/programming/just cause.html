About This Game

The Mediterranean republic of Medici is suffering under the brutal control of General Di Ravello, a dictator with an insatiable appetite for power. Enter Rico Rodriguez, a man on a mission to destroy the General’s hold on power by any means necessary. With over 400 square miles of complete freedom from sky to seabed and a huge arsenal of weaponry, gadgets and vehicles, prepare to unleash chaos in the most creative and explosive ways you can imagine.

FEATURES:

  • Explore a Mediterranean island paradise with complete vertical freedom – skydive, BASE jump and free dive in an open world with virtually zero limits
  • Glide through the air and swoop across mountains with your Wingsuit giving a new way to rain death from above
  • Use your Grapple and Parachute to scale buildings, hijack vehicles, move quickly or tether objects together for creative new ways to cause Chaos.
  • Cause massive chains of destruction in military bases, harbours, prisons, police stations and communications facilities to bring down a dictator
  • Arm yourself with a wide range of explosive weaponry from shotguns and missile launchers to tank-busters and air-strikes
  • Choose from a huge variety of different vehicles to drive including speedboats, jets, helicopters, turbo-fuelled sports cars and super bikes
  • Get adventurous with dozens of challenge missions and collectibles to discover
  • Online community features

3/18/2016 *SKY FORTRESS BELOW*

My rating 7/10

Pros:
-Explosions
-Removed a lot of pointless mechanics from JC2, namely pressing number sequences every time you wanted to hijack an enemy vehicle, or hack a terminal. Also like a 10 second cutscene everytime you wanted to fast travel or get a weapons drop
-Heavy weapons that explode things in different ways
-graphics are pretty
-lots of collectables, one of them being audio tapes that give some backstory to the main antagonist's rise to power
-The trains are awesome and I spent a lot of time causing crashes and derails. (WHY IS THERE ONLY ONE TRAIN MISSION? SO MUCH POTENTIAL THERE)
-Rebel vs Army battles (only 3 in the game sadly) Fighting 10 enemy tanks in your own tank is pretty fun

The leaderboards and challegenes are pretty controversial, mainly for being psudo multiplayer. (The JC2 multiplayer mod was seen as welcome addition and left people wondering why it wasn't included in the game.)
It seems the devs just got bored and said "players like multiplayer so lets give them arbitrary leaderboards"

Cons
-Liberating settlements is cookie cutter as ????. For towns theres almost always a police station to liberate and a handfull of propaganda structures to destroy just like every other city in the game.
-Enemy AI could use some work. For example if you hover in a helicopter near a cliff the AI will just drive off the cliff to their deaths trying to get to you.
-Cutscenes, why not let me crash the evil missile instead of watching Rico do it in a cutscene?
-Story missions are bland and really easy.
-No cool landmarks like the night club balloon, the highrise cities, and the highrise towers in JC2. No diversity in what the towns and cities look like.
-Only a handful of sports cars that go over 160 km/h
-SAM trailers and bridges regenerate if you leave the area (more specifically when you leave rendering distance in a helicopter or jet, fly up too high and the bridge comes back)
-Cool gameplay features like battles and the train mission are few and limited.
-No tanks with tracks they all have wheels. :)

TL/DR if you enjoyed JC2, you will feel right at home with this game. If you want a sandbox to blow everything up wait for the game to go on sale (and more DLC).

Not as bad as you may expect

Seems JC3 has received some pretty much hate since release.
But I just lowered my expectations, and took a leap of faith.
No regrets yet.
It's the most silly, mindless, unrealistic ride of destryoing things since "Red Faction: Guerilla" (which tried to be serious though).
JustCause is to the FarCry-Series like "Saints Row" is to "GTA": It's its mad brother. You can't trust him, he's abusing drugs and hits on your wife (or hubby). But he's more fun at parties than accountant-brother.

The good (what to love):
  • It's just "Just Cause 2" but more of the lovely stuff, even less serious, and even more hillarious physics
  • The new tethering-stuff is an awesome pool of crazy, twisted and even horribly mean fun
  • The wingsuit/parasuit enable for nearly endless flying
  • Blowing up stuff has never been so much fun. Place C4 at the right spots, detonate them, watch all come down.
  • Realism? Not in the slightest!
  • Leaderboards with a very nice feature: Race the ghost of your friends (or others). You select someone else from the leaderboards, and start the challenge. It's like multiplayer but solo :-)
  • Mods (though not officially and only a handful right now, but some more will surely come)
  • Performs pretty well and still looks decent for 2015 (yes I know, seems I'm lucky here)
  • Better (but still not perfect) car-handling
  • Beautiful italian setting
  • Large open-world fun
  • Collectibles like audiotapes, cards, vintage-parts. Enough to keep collectors busy
  • Want to punish your minions? Tether to feet and tower. Retract. Laugh evilish. If there would be any limb separation you could quarter them with 4 tethers :-)

The mediocre (not good, not bad, but no show-stopper either):
  • Challenges. You don't HAVE to do them, but if you want all those gadgets and goodies and abilities, you'll sadly have to do them. And win them if possible (5-Star). Funny for the first time but some tend to be frustratingly hard. At least for me :-)
  • After around 15hrs I only had one bug where the car I was driving suddenly dissapeared. Been more funny than annoying though. Beside this I only had very minor glitches with the tethering.
  • Stupid, unskippable lengthy intro.
    (Edit: Thanks to mods, you now can...)
  • Animals are pathetic. One wrong look and they drop dead. Walked into a deer. Deer dropped dead. Walked into a goat. Goat dropped dead.

The ugly (what to hate, may kill the fun):
  • Enemies spawn. Sometimes you kill two guards on the 5m² top of a damn high tower, turn around and there they are again. HORRIBLE, ANNOYING and a real showstopper
  • Season-pass-bullcrap again. Please stop this pandemic brainfart
  • Killing EVERYONE does not matter at all. Kill a dozen rebels in front of other rebels and they still yell "Yay! Rico! Make me babies! Prego!"
  • Though I can't complain at all, according to the mixed reviews and general critics, it has many bugs, flaws and runs crappy on AMD
  • Can't remap controller but keyboard. Half-done job


Recommended?

If you liked JC1+2 and enjoy the mindless, phsyics-defying destruction-rampage: YES.
If you already found JC2 to be too silly, unbelievable and unrealistic: HELL NO!

It may not be perfect, it may have some problems, but beside the spawning-horror and the more-or-less forced challenges it's a damn funny and mindless ride!
I cannot understand the "mixed" ratings except they're mostly because of the technical problems it may have (which I cannot judge). It's a worthy successor in a
beautiful environment and it "upped" Rico from a guy with a grappling-hook to basically Ironman without the suit. It sometimes even felt like "Prototype" :-)

As far as I am in this game: Not one crash, no annoying glitches, no game-breaking bugs. Had expected much worse!

According to other reviews, you may wait for at least the first patch if you own any AMD-Hardware though :(

My take after 5 hours... I would both recommend and also discourage people to buy/not buy this, but I could only chose one option so please read on to see why!


Performance - Does it run?

After an initial drama with getting it to run at my native resolution - the game crashed every time I switched res and eventually I had to change it in the config file to get it to load - on my machine yes it does, and very very well.. However I'm lucky enough to have just upgraded to the following:

i7 6700k @ Stock 4.0GHz
Asus Maximus VIII Hero Mobo
16GB G.Skill Ripjaws V 3000MHz
MSI GTX 980Ti Gaming 6G

Now I realise this is at the upper end of the hardware scale, and if you're in a position similar to me you can expect to be able to play at a minimum of 100fps (average of ~130) on 1080p with every setting on maximum, so I'm expecting 80-90 on 1440p when I get a new monitor in a couple of weeks (will update with the actual figure).

The game seems to be a bit of a memory hog and while playing it my system uses about 8.2GB with just the game and a few google chrome tabs open, so this could potentially be an issue for people with less.

As others have commented, the initial game loading times are quite slow (using an SSD), however once the map is loaded in, the missions etc load very quickly.


Gameplay / Feel

I've only played 5 hours but this is my 2c so far..

The game looks jaw dropping and if I'm honest is half the reason I bought it, to see what my new system was capable of. I messed around a bit with lowering some of the graphic settings and with a lot of them the quality of the game doesn't decrease too much and you can gain a few fps by doing this, so I wouldn't be worried about how its going to look if you play at medium etc - it will still be incredibly pretty.

After getting over the initial oooooo this is pretty thoughts, the actual game itself is fun in the respect of being able to mindlessly blow things up and fly about like a super-spider-bat-man kind of combo thing (it's bizarre!) It's fun jumping in a tank and blowing up a petrol station, or in a helicopter that has infinite spammable missiles, or planting mines on a bridge, then causing a pile up before blowing the whole thing up etc etc...

Regarding the campaign / story, well to me (admittedly a Just Cause noob) the premise is a bit thin.. a dictator has gone a bit mental and therefore we need to crush him and his minions by taking over towns, then more towns, and a few more, and then a couple more after that.. you get the point. These towns are all just clones of one another and there are only so many "fun and creative" ways to shoot, blow up, or pull down yet another identical red billboard or telegraph pole in order to take over the 100000th one.

I know already I wont be able to stomach completing the story as its simply just too damn repetitive. The side missions to upgrade your perks etc are sort of fun, but really not challenging enough to take seriously.

The cars in the game are HORRENDOUS to control, by either a keyboard or a controller, they just plain suck. The heli's are much more fun, but a bit too EZ-MODE. I've had the most fun just cruising around on my parachute & wingsuit (which is a good laugh when you get the hang of it) and getting up to random mischief - such as slingshotting people with the tether device combined with booster bombs and seeing how far someone can fly without a parachute! Again though, this will only last so long, and after it wears thin I probably wont log back in as it just doesn't have enough pull to keep me interested.


Conclusion

+If you have some decent PC hardware (that doesn't include an AMD gpu (sorry guys!)) then the game *should* run ok, and hopefully even better after a patch. If this is the case for you, you will be treated to a super pretty game with a MASSIVE world to go mental in.
+If you are the type of person to roam about on GTA and just randomly cause chaos you will no doubt love this game, as nothing will compare to the madness you can create.
+If you're the type of person who enjoys a good story with engaging, challenging and varied game play too, you should probably pass at least until its in a steam sale, as you will soon find it tedious and frustratingly repetitive for the £40 price tag!


I hope this has helped at least someone! Please comment below if you found this useful, or alternatively crap! Please feel free to let me know what I could have done to improve this, if anything!

Thumbs up if you like and down if you don't!

Cheers.
